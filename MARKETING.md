# Marketing Plan: Grant Writing Assistant

## Marketplace Copy

**Headline:** Write Winning Grant Proposals in Hours — Not Weeks

**Subheadline:** 4 prompts that replace $5,000–$15,000 in grant consulting fees

**Short description (150 words):**
Grant consultants charge $5K–$15K per proposal. Most nonprofits and small businesses can't afford that — so they write weak applications, miss deadlines, or give up entirely. 1.5 million US nonprofits leave billions in unclaimed grant funding on the table every year.

This skill changes that. Four structured prompts generate a complete, submission-ready grant application: opportunity research with fit scoring (so you pursue the right grants), executive summary and needs statement (the sections reviewers weight most), project narrative with month-by-month work plan, and evaluation framework with full budget justification.

Built on the same frameworks professional grant writers use — logic models, SMART objectives, cost-effectiveness calculations, and evidence-based narrative structure. The difference: $29 instead of $15,000.

**Category:** Business / Nonprofit  
**Tags:** grant-writing, nonprofit, fundraising, proposal, SBIR, foundation

---

## Target Buyers

### Primary: Nonprofit Program Directors / Executive Directors
- **Size:** 1.5M+ US nonprofits, 200K+ actively seeking grants
- **Pain:** Writing grants takes 40-80 hours per application; staff time is scarce
- **Trigger:** Grant deadline approaching, new program needs funding, previous application rejected
- **Where they are:** GrantSpace, Foundation Center forums, AFP (Association of Fundraising Professionals), LinkedIn nonprofit groups
- **Willingness to pay:** High — a $29 skill that saves 20+ hours has obvious ROI

### Secondary: Small Business Owners (SBIR/STTR applicants)
- **Size:** 300K+ SBIR applications submitted annually across 11 federal agencies
- **Pain:** Federal grant applications are complex; hired consultants are expensive
- **Trigger:** R&D phase, DOD/NIH/DOE solicitation released, startup seeking non-dilutive funding
- **Where they are:** SBIR.gov forums, startup accelerator Slack groups, founder communities

### Tertiary: Freelance Grant Writers
- **Size:** 50K+ independent grant writers in the US
- **Pain:** Client volume exceeds capacity; proposal quality varies with time pressure
- **Trigger:** New client onboarding, scaling service capacity, improving win rate
- **Willingness to pay:** Very high — they bill $5K-$15K/proposal; tool that cuts time in half is obvious value

### Quaternary: Academic Researchers
- **Size:** 800K+ researchers at US universities seeking extramural funding
- **Pain:** Research time is precious; grant writing is a different skill set
- **Trigger:** NSF, NIH, NEH solicitation; tenure review requiring external funding

## Pricing Strategy

### Skill Price: $29 one-time
- Justified: saves 20-40 hours of staff time (nonprofit avg hourly cost: $25-35/hr → $500-$1,400 in time savings)
- Compared to: $5K-$15K consultants, $200-$2K freelancers
- Conversion trigger: one successful grant at even $5,000 = 172x ROI on the skill

### DFY Service: $297/application
- Max runs all 4 prompts with client's details
- Formats into submission-ready Google Doc (executive summary, narrative, budget, evaluation plan)
- Delivery: 48-72 hours
- Client provides: mission, program details, budget, specific grant information
- Margin: ~2-3 hours of Max's time → ~$100-150/hr effective rate
- Upsell: $97 revision round if needed

### Monthly Retainer: $497/month
- Up to 3 applications/month
- Grant calendar management (tracking deadlines)
- Opportunity sourcing (Prompt 1 run monthly for new prospects)
- Target: grant writers scaling a practice, orgs with active funding pipelines
